CSIT6000N Critical Thinking Competition

2023-02-13

After reflecting on the previous essay and what I have learned this semester, I still believe that the first-generation model Apple Pencil's design is bad in addressing some of the charging issues. In addition to the user's perspective, my study of human-computer interaction this semester has provided me with a fresh perspective on the challenges associated with charging the device.
To assess the design's effectiveness in meeting user expectations, I have employed Usability Testing to consider the user's viewpoint. A key issue with charging the Pencil is that people often forget to do so until it is needed, causing inconvenience. By establishing a deep empathy with users, designers can develop innovative solutions.
To explore potential remedies, I consider the Past, Present, and Future of charging technologies to think of possible solutions. Looking back at the past, the method of charging devices was often limited to contact-based solutions. However, in the present day, we have seen the emergence of non-contact charging methods that have revolutionized the way we power our devices. These new technologies have enabled us to charge our devices without the need for physical contact, allowing for greater convenience and flexibility.
In response to the challenge of charging the Apple Pencil, designers have leveraged the needs of users and the possibilities of technology to create innovative solutions for business success. For example, the second-generation Apple Pencil incorporates magnetic properties, allowing it to charge effortlessly when connected to the iPad. This feature ensures users always have a charged device at their disposal, even if they forget to charge it beforehand. By removing the need for users to remember to charge their Pencil in advance, this design solution simplifies the user experience and boosts productivity.
In conclusion, although the second-generation Apple Pencil has addressed some of the charging issues, its limited compatibility makes it an impractical choice for many iPad users. A better design for a stylus would prioritize convenience and usability for all users.
